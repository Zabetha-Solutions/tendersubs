Sector guides

Know what's expected before you submit

Each sector has its own compliance documents and evaluation focus. Use these guides to prepare, then head to the submission form.

Government

National & provincial department tenders

Government tenders are typically the most document-intensive, with strict compliance and BBBEE requirements. Late or incomplete submissions are usually disqualified outright, so completeness matters more than speed.

Typical documents required

  • Company registration (CIPC) documents
  • Valid tax clearance / SARS PIN
  • BBBEE certificate or sworn affidavit
  • Completed SBD forms (SBD1, SBD4, SBD6.1, SBD8, SBD9)
  • Pricing schedule
  • Proof of banking details

Municipal

City & local municipality tenders

Municipal tenders focus on local economic development — expect requirements around local labour, SMME participation and rates compliance in addition to standard registration documents.

Typical documents required

  • Municipal supplier / vendor registration
  • Rates and municipal accounts clearance certificate
  • Local labour & SMME participation declaration
  • Tax clearance certificate
  • Company organogram & capacity statement

Construction

Civil, building & infrastructure tenders

Construction tenders evaluate technical capability alongside price — method statements, programmes and safety records carry real weight in adjudication.

Typical documents required

  • CIDB grading / registration certificate
  • Method statement and construction programme
  • Health & safety file and OHS compliance
  • Proof of plant, equipment & personnel
  • References from comparable projects

Mining

Mining, exploration & supply-chain tenders

Mining tenders place heavy emphasis on safety, environmental compliance and specialised equipment or competency records, often alongside site-specific inductions.

Typical documents required

  • Mine Health & Safety Act compliance documentation
  • Environmental management plan / authorisation
  • Equipment specifications & maintenance records
  • Competency certificates for specialised personnel
  • Public liability & insurance certificates

Private Sector

Corporate RFPs & vendor onboarding

Private-sector tenders vary the most by client — requirements are set by the issuing company rather than statute, so read the RFP closely and tailor your scope-of-work response.

Typical documents required

  • Company profile & client references
  • Proof of insurance / professional indemnity cover
  • Scope-of-work response & proposed pricing
  • Banking & vendor onboarding details
  • Any client-specific compliance forms
